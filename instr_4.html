Here are some helpful advices after the day of vaccination
Can i resume my normal activities after vaccination against COVID-19?
Yes, you can continue to participate in all of them your planned activities, as long as you feel healthy. For more information you can refer to website of the Ministry of Health: emvolio.gov.gr
Can there be any side effects after vaccination?
According to clinical studies, the most common side effects actions that may occur after vaccination are pain injection site, headache, myalgias, arthralgias, fatigue, chills or fever. These symptoms may be more common in younger ages. They recede soon and you do not have to worry, because it is an indication that your body is responding to the vaccine. Taking antipyretic and anti-inflammatory drugs helps address them.If symptoms persist, contact your doctor or vaccination center. If the if you wish, you can report side effects that you consider related to the administration of the COVID-19 vaccine on the page of the National Medicines Agency, completing the Yellow Card (www.eof.gr/web/guest/yellowgeneral).
Am I protected from COVID-19 now that I have been vaccinated?
Most vaccines for COVID-19 need two doses to are effective. Each vaccine has been tested in studies before being approved involving more than 30,000 volunteers and has has been shown to significantly reduce the chances of getting seriously ill by COVID-19. It takes several days after administration second dose of the vaccine to develop our body immune to the virus. Some people may get sick from despite being vaccinated COVID-19, but their disease is expected to be much milder.
What should I do after the first dose of the vaccine?
You need to make sure your vaccination is repeated inside within the prescribed time, as the second dose of the vaccine is necessary for the development of protection against the virus.
Will I continue to need personal protection measures afterwards vaccination?
After vaccination we must continue to adhere to the measures protection such as hand hygiene, mask use and adherence distances. This is because no vaccine is 100% effective and the virus will continue to circulate in the community until vaccinated a large percentage of the population. The higher the percentage of the population vaccinated, the sooner it will be reduced the circulation of the virus in the community.
